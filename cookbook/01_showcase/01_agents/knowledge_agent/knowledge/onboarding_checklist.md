# New Hire Onboarding Checklist

## Before Your First Day

Your manager and HR will help you complete these items before you start:

- [ ] Sign offer letter and employment documents
- [ ] Complete background check
- [ ] Submit direct deposit information
- [ ] Choose health insurance plan (if applicable)
- [ ] Receive laptop shipping confirmation

## Week 1: Getting Started

### Day 1

- [ ] Attend new hire orientation (9 AM)
- [ ] Complete I-9 verification with HR
- [ ] Set up company email and accounts
- [ ] Meet your manager and team
- [ ] Review team goals and your role
- [ ] Set up Slack and join required channels:
  - #general
  - #your-team-name
  - #engineering (if applicable)
  - #random

### Day 2-3

- [ ] Complete required compliance training:
  - Security awareness training
  - Anti-harassment training
  - Data privacy training
- [ ] Set up development environment (engineering)
- [ ] Review team documentation and wiki
- [ ] Schedule 1:1s with key stakeholders

### Day 4-5

- [ ] Complete first small task or project
- [ ] Attend team standup meetings
- [ ] Review benefits enrollment (due within 30 days)
- [ ] Meet your onboarding buddy

## Week 2: Deepening Knowledge

- [ ] Continue development environment setup
- [ ] Review codebase and architecture (engineering)
- [ ] Complete product training
- [ ] Shadow team members on common tasks
- [ ] Start contributing to team projects
- [ ] Schedule coffee chats with cross-functional partners

## Month 1: Building Momentum

- [ ] Complete all compliance training modules
- [ ] Finish benefits enrollment
- [ ] Complete first meaningful contribution
- [ ] Attend monthly all-hands meeting
- [ ] Have 30-day check-in with manager
- [ ] Review and understand OKRs

## Month 2-3: Ramping Up

- [ ] Take ownership of a feature or project
- [ ] Present at team meeting
- [ ] Participate in code reviews (engineering)
- [ ] Complete on-call training (if applicable)
- [ ] Have 60-day check-in with manager
- [ ] Start thinking about goals for the year

## Tips for Success

1. **Ask questions** - There are no dumb questions during onboarding
2. **Take notes** - You'll learn a lot in the first few weeks
3. **Meet people** - Schedule coffee chats to build relationships
4. **Be patient** - It takes time to ramp up, and that's okay
5. **Give feedback** - Help us improve the onboarding experience

## Manager Responsibilities

Your manager should:
- [ ] Welcome you on day 1
- [ ] Set up recurring 1:1s
- [ ] Provide clear first-week tasks
- [ ] Assign an onboarding buddy
- [ ] Schedule introductions with key stakeholders
- [ ] Set 30/60/90 day expectations
- [ ] Check in regularly during first month

## Onboarding Buddy

Your onboarding buddy is there to help you:
- Navigate the company and team
- Answer informal questions
- Introduce you to team culture
- Provide context on how things work
